Buying a house remotely is complicated. Buying raw land remotely is a different challenge entirely — and one that trips up buyers who don't know what they're doing.

I've helped clients purchase bare land in North Idaho from across the country. Here's what I've learned about doing it right.

## Why Land Is Different

With a house, you can lean on the home inspection to surface most issues. With raw land, there's no inspector who reviews the whole property in a standardized way. You're responsible for understanding:

- **Access:** Does the parcel have legal, deeded access? Is the road maintained year-round or seasonal?
- **Utilities:** Is the land on the power grid? Does water require a well? What's the distance to sewer connections, and is that distance feasible?
- **Zoning and covenants:** What can you legally build on this land? Are there HOA covenants, easements, or restrictions that affect your plans?
- **Topography and buildability:** Is the lot actually buildable given the slope, soil, and floodplain status?

These aren't things you can determine from a listing photo. They require investigation.

## The Out-of-State Challenge

When you're not local, you can't just drive by. You can't walk the property on a Tuesday afternoon before making an offer. You're relying heavily on your agent to be your eyes, ears, and investigator.

This is where representation matters more than almost anywhere else in real estate. An agent who doesn't flag access issues, who doesn't verify utilities, who doesn't review the parcel map carefully — that agent costs you money. Potentially a lot of money.

## What We Do Differently

When I work with out-of-state land buyers, I treat the property like I'm buying it myself. That means thorough documentation before you commit — photos and video from multiple angles, verification of access, preliminary conversations with county planning about buildability, and a complete package of information so you can make a real decision.

Every document that needs your signature gets to you in time. Every question that needs answering gets answered before closing — not after.
