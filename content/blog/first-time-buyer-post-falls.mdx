Buying your first home is one of the most significant things you'll ever do — and one of the most confusing, if you don't have someone to walk you through it. That's what this post is for.

I've worked with dozens of first-time buyers across Post Falls and North Idaho, and the questions I hear most often are almost always the same. Let me answer them directly.

## Start with Pre-Approval — Before You Look at a Single House

I know it's tempting to browse Zillow for months before talking to a lender. I get it. But here's the truth: until you have a pre-approval letter, you're shopping in the abstract.

Pre-approval tells you:
- **Exactly what you can afford** (not an estimate — a real number)
- **Which loan types you qualify for** (conventional, FHA, USDA — all different down payment requirements)
- **How much you'll actually spend monthly** on your mortgage, taxes, and insurance combined

In Post Falls, a good home in the $300-400K range can receive multiple offers within days of listing. If you don't have pre-approval, you can't move fast enough when the right home shows up.

## The Post Falls Market: What First-Time Buyers Need to Know

Post Falls is one of North Idaho's most accessible markets — which is part of why it's so competitive. You're getting proximity to Coeur d'Alene's beauty and amenities, without CDA's price premium.

A few things to know:
- **Inventory moves quickly.** New listings at good price points get attention fast.
- **Some loan types are limited.** If you're using FHA or USDA financing, the home needs to meet certain condition standards — older homes or properties with deferred maintenance may not qualify without repairs.
- **Budget for closing costs.** Beyond your down payment, plan for 2-4% of the purchase price in closing costs. We can sometimes negotiate seller concessions to help offset this.

## What the Process Actually Looks Like

Here's a realistic timeline for a first-time buyer in the current North Idaho market:

**Week 1-2:** Phone call with me, connect with a lender, start pre-approval process.

**Week 2-4:** Get pre-approved. Start touring homes together.

**Week 4-8:** Find the right home (timelines vary — some clients find it in a week, some take 6-8 weeks). Submit an offer.

**Day 1-3 after offer:** Offer accepted, negotiate if needed, sign purchase agreement.

**Days 4-10:** Schedule and complete home inspection. Review results together.

**Days 10-30:** Appraisal, final loan approval, clear-to-close.

**Closing day:** Sign paperwork. Get your keys. Try not to cry (I always tear up a little).

## The Questions You're Afraid to Ask

**"Is this a bad time to buy?"**
It's never a perfect time to buy a home. Rates go up and down. Markets go up and down. The best time to buy is when it makes financial sense for you and when you plan to stay put for at least 3-5 years.

**"What if I find a problem during inspection?"**
That's exactly what inspections are for. We'll review the report together and decide whether to ask the seller to repair items, credit you money at closing, or in rare cases, walk away. You're protected.

**"Can I really afford the homes in Post Falls?"**
Maybe more than you think. There are still deals to be found, especially for buyers who are flexible on timing and location. Let's talk about what your budget actually gets you.
