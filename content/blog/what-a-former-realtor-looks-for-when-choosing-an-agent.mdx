The hardest clients to earn — and the most meaningful to have — are the ones who know exactly what good looks like.

Former real estate professionals are in that category. They've seen agents at their best and at their worst. They know the shortcuts some agents take. They know what "above and beyond" actually requires, because they've done it themselves.

When a former REALTOR® chooses to work with you, it means something.

## What the Insiders Look For

Here's what experienced real estate professionals evaluate when they're the ones hiring an agent:

**1. Communication that doesn't require chasing.** Good agents don't need to be followed up with. They send updates before you have to ask. They answer calls. They explain what's happening and what's coming next.

**2. Honest pricing, not flattery.** An agent who tells you what you want to hear to win the listing will cost you money. The right agent tells you what the market actually says and backs it up with data.

**3. Diligence with the details.** Real estate transactions fail because of small things. Missed deadlines. Unsigned addenda. Contingencies that lapse. The best agents build systems to make sure none of that happens.

**4. A real network.** Not a brand name — actual relationships. Agents they can call when a listing hits. Lenders who pick up the phone. Title reps who know how to solve problems at the last minute.

**5. Someone who actually cares.** This one's harder to quantify, but former professionals recognize it immediately. There's a difference between an agent who's motivated by commission and one who's motivated by the outcome for their client.

## What It Looks Like When These Things Are Present

Transactions that close on time. Clients who feel informed and supported throughout. Results that hold up when you compare them to the market.

That's what I aim for with every client — whether they're a first-time buyer, a seasoned seller, or a former real estate professional who knows exactly what to look for.
