Every year, thousands of people make the decision to move to North Idaho. Some come from Seattle. Some from California. Some from across the country entirely. And a significant number of them have to buy a home before they've officially made the move.

I've helped many out-of-state buyers navigate this process. Here's what you need to know.

## The Challenges Are Real — and Solvable

Buying a home remotely means:

- You can't tour every property you're interested in
- You can't get the feel of a neighborhood without being in it
- You're making a major financial decision with less information than a local buyer
- Timing across time zones complicates communication

These are real challenges. They're also solvable with the right system and the right agent.

## Video Tours Are Not Optional

Every property you're seriously considering should have a live video walkthrough — not just listing photos, not just a recorded virtual tour. A real-time call where you can ask questions, have me point the camera where you want it, open doors, check the light, look at the neighborhood.

I treat video tours like a first showing. They're detailed, thorough, and honest.

## Neighborhood Context Matters More Than You Think

Listings don't tell you that a neighborhood is quiet, or that there's a train that passes twice a day, or that the street floods in spring. Local knowledge is the part of real estate that doesn't transfer from a screen.

When you work with me, you get my honest read of every neighborhood, every street, every property. Not a sales pitch — actual context that helps you make the right decision.

## Logistics: Getting Documents Signed, Inspections Managed, and Closing Coordinated

Remote transactions require extra coordination. Digital signatures make the document side manageable. For inspections, I attend in person, take detailed notes, and walk you through everything on a call.

For closing, remote notarization and mobile notaries make it possible to close from wherever you are without being here in person.
