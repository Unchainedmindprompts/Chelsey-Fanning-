There's a version of real estate that's purely transactional. You hire an agent, they submit paperwork, you close, everyone moves on. The job technically gets done.

Then there's a different version — where the experience itself is something you remember and talk about years later. Where it feels like someone actually cared.

The difference usually comes down to the small things.

## What "Above and Beyond" Actually Looks Like

In real estate, going above and beyond isn't usually about dramatic gestures. It's about the details that most agents don't bother with because nobody's keeping score.

It's showing up to every showing prepared. It's sending the follow-up email after inspection without waiting to be asked. It's remembering that your client mentioned they're nervous about the appraisal and checking in the morning it's scheduled.

It's a welcome home gift on closing day — not because you're required to, but because this is a moment worth marking.

## Why the Small Things Matter

Real estate transactions are stressful by nature. There are a lot of unknowns. A lot of waiting. A lot of decisions under pressure with imperfect information.

The small things reduce that stress. They signal to clients that someone is paying attention, that nothing is falling through the cracks, that they're in good hands.

And when the transaction is over and someone asks your client whether they'd recommend their agent — the answer is shaped as much by how the experience felt as by what the outcome was.

## The Standard I Hold Myself To

Every client I work with deserves to feel like a priority — not just on the big days, but throughout. That's the standard I hold myself to, and it's the reason clients come back and send me their family and friends.
