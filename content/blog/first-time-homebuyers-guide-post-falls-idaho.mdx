Buying your first home is one of the biggest decisions of your life — and one of the most confusing, if you don't have someone walking you through it step by step. I've helped dozens of first-time buyers across Post Falls and North Idaho, and the same questions come up every time. Let me answer them directly.

## Step 1: Start with Pre-Approval — Before You Look at a Single House

I know it's tempting to browse Zillow for months before talking to a lender. But until you have a pre-approval letter, you're shopping in the abstract. Pre-approval tells you exactly what you can afford, which loan types you qualify for, and what your real monthly payment will be.

In Post Falls, well-priced homes in the $300–500K range can receive multiple offers within days. If you don't have pre-approval, you can't move fast enough.

## Step 2: Understand the Post Falls Market

Post Falls is one of North Idaho's most accessible markets. You get Coeur d'Alene's beauty and amenities without the CDA price premium. A few things first-time buyers need to know:

- **Inventory moves quickly.** New listings at good price points get attention fast.
- **FHA and USDA loans have condition requirements.** Older homes with deferred maintenance may not qualify without repairs.
- **Budget for closing costs.** Plan for 2–4% of the purchase price beyond your down payment. We can sometimes negotiate seller concessions to offset this.

## What the Process Actually Looks Like

**Weeks 1–2:** Phone call with me, connect with a lender, begin pre-approval.

**Weeks 2–4:** Get pre-approved. Start touring homes together.

**Weeks 4–8:** Find the right home (some clients find it in a week, others take longer). Submit an offer.

**Days 1–3 after offer accepted:** Sign purchase agreement.

**Days 4–10:** Home inspection. Review results together — repair requests, credits, or rarely, walking away.

**Days 10–30:** Appraisal, final loan approval, clear-to-close.

**Closing day:** Sign paperwork. Get your keys.

## The Questions You're Afraid to Ask

**"Is this a bad time to buy?"** It's never a perfect time. The best time to buy is when it makes financial sense for you and when you plan to stay for at least 3–5 years.

**"What if inspection finds problems?"** That's exactly what it's for. We'll review together and decide how to respond. You're protected.

**"Can I actually afford homes in Post Falls?"** More than you might think. Let's talk about what your budget actually gets you.
