Coeur d'Alene is one of the most desirable real estate markets in the entire country. Sellers here are in a good position — but "good market" doesn't mean you can skip the fundamentals. Here's what I tell every seller I work with before we put a sign in the yard.

## 1. Price It Right from Day One

This is the single biggest mistake sellers make. In a strong market, there's a temptation to test the ceiling — to list above comparable sales and see what happens. This strategy almost always backfires.

Here's why: homes that sit on market too long develop a stigma. Buyers start asking, "What's wrong with it?" The longer you're listed, the more leverage shifts to buyers. And when you eventually reduce the price, you often end up accepting less than if you'd priced it correctly from the start.

The right price isn't the highest price you can imagine — it's the price that creates demand, generates offers, and gets you to closing at maximum value. Those are two different things, and I'll help you understand the difference.

## 2. First Impressions Happen Online, Not at the Door

Over 95% of home buyers start their search online. The first time a buyer sees your home isn't when they pull up to the driveway — it's when your photos load on their phone at 10pm on a Tuesday.

This means:
- **Professional photography is mandatory**, not optional
- **The lead photo** (the exterior or hero shot) makes or breaks whether buyers click through
- **Clutter, dated staging, and bad lighting** will cost you more money than quality photography ever would

I work with professional photographers who know how to make North Idaho homes look their best. This is a non-negotiable part of my listings.

## 3. Disclosure Is Not Optional — And It Protects You

Idaho law requires sellers to disclose known material defects in a property. This is both a legal obligation and a practical protection for you.

The instinct to hide or downplay issues is understandable — no seller wants to scare buyers away. But undisclosed issues that come up during inspection (or worse, after closing) can result in legal liability, failed deals, and lost time.

My advice: disclose, and disclose thoroughly. Buyers respect transparency. A disclosed issue that's priced correctly is far less damaging than one that comes as a surprise.

## 4. Your Agent's Network Matters More Than Their Signs

A big real estate brand name on a sign doesn't automatically mean your home gets more exposure. What matters is whether your agent has real relationships with other local agents, active buyers in their pipeline, and the ability to market your property through more channels than a basic MLS listing.

When I list a property, I personally reach out to agents I know are actively working with buyers in your price range. I use social media strategically — not just posting, but targeting. The goal is to find the right buyer, not just post and pray.

## 5. Be Realistic About Your Timeline

In a healthy market, a well-priced and well-presented Coeur d'Alene home can receive offers within days. But "selling fast" isn't always the same as "selling well." Here's what a realistic timeline looks like:

- **2-3 weeks** to prepare the home, photography, and marketing materials
- **1-4 weeks** on the market before an accepted offer (faster for well-priced homes)
- **30-45 days** from accepted offer to closing (depending on buyer financing)

If you need to close by a specific date, tell me upfront and we'll build the strategy around that.
